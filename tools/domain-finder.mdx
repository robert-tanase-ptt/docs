## Overview

The Domain Finder discovers domains related to your target organization, including acquisitions, subsidiaries, and alternate brands. Understanding the full domain portfolio is essential for comprehensive security assessments.

## What it finds

- Related company domains
- Alternate TLDs (.com, .net, .org, .io, etc.)
- Regional domain variations
- Historical domains
- Subsidiary and acquisition domains
- Brand variation domains

## How to use

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter seed domain">
    Provide a known domain for the target organization.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure options">
    Set search parameters and depth.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run discovery">
    Execute the domain finding process.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review results">
    Analyze discovered domains and verify relevance.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Discovery methods

| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **WHOIS analysis** | Find domains with similar registration data |
| **Certificate search** | Discover domains in SSL certificates |
| **Reverse DNS** | Identify domains on shared infrastructure |
| **Pattern matching** | Find variations of known domains |

## Results

For each discovered domain:
- Domain name
- Registration status
- Discovery method
- Relationship to seed domain

<Note>
Not all discovered domains may belong to your target. Always verify ownership before adding to testing scope.
</Note>

## Following up

After discovering related domains:
1. **Verify ownership** — Confirm domains belong to the target
2. **Run subdomain enumeration** — Use [Subdomain Finder](/tools/subdomain-finder)
3. **Check for takeover risks** — Use [Subdomain Takeover](/tools/subdomain-takeover)
4. **Scan discovered assets** — Run appropriate scanners
